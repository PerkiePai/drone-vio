Datasets

MARS-LVIG — nadir aerial, smooth cruise, repetitive canopy (2448×2048)
TUM-VI room1 — handheld, fast rotation, rich indoor texture (512×512)

Metrics

Mean matches — average over all pairs
Min matches — worst-case pair (survival floor)
Inlier ratio — RANSAC fundamental matrix
Track survival — features seeded frame 0, chained consecutively

VIO constraint

Factor graph needs ≥ 15 correspondences per frame to stay alive. Min matches and survival are the critical numbers — mean can look fine while one bad frame kills the estimator.

Stage 1

Three Pipelines

Same 640×480 pixels fed to all. Latency = warmed-up average.

SuperGlue

SuperPoint → GNN matcher. Weights bound to SuperPoint — no extractor swap.

LightGlue

SuperPoint → attention matcher, adaptive depth. Faster than SuperGlue.

XFeat

Single CNN: detect + 64-d describe + MNN match. No SuperPoint dependency.

Stage 3

Adaptive Confidence (XFeat+LGdyn)

Two-tier strategy to guarantee ≥15 matches even on hard frames.

Step 1 — Normal (easy frames)

Match at min_conf = 0.1 (tight). Zero extra cost — same as static.

Step 2 — Rescue (count < 15)

Re-match at min_conf = 0.02 (loose). Accept lower-quality matches to keep factor graph alive.

KLT — best for nadir cruise

Highest mean count, 98% survival over 30 frames. Breaks catastrophically on fast motion (min=1 at gap 10). Ideal native regime: slow smooth flight.

ALIKED+LG — most robust overall

99.8% inlier ratio on nadir, stable floor (501 at gap 10), holds through fast motion. 3 ms matcher latency. Best general-purpose VIO front-end.

XFeat+LGdyn — pairwise rescue only

Highest raw count on TUM-VI, good pairwise floor on fast motion. But 65% inlier ratio on nadir and track chain dead by frame 18. Re-detection architecture incompatible with long MSCKF tracks.

Recommendation: ALIKED+LightGlue as the primary VIO front-end. Matches at ~3 ms; 99.8% inlier ratio on nadir; floor never drops below VIO minimum. KLT as a secondary/hybrid for smooth cruise segments. XFeat+LGdyn is not suitable as a VIO tracker — re-detection kills long-chain survival which MSCKF depends on.
