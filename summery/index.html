The Hard Part

High-altitude nadir imagery = near-zero parallax. Standard monocular VIO scale recovery relies on IMU excitation × visible parallax. Above ~20 m over flat terrain, scale becomes unobservable.

Core Challenge

Why Monocular VIO Fails at Altitude

Two independent scale mechanisms — both break at altitude for different reasons.

IMU-based scale — breaks above ~20 m (parallax vanishes)

Depth-based scale — breaks over non-flat terrain

Path 1: IMU-scale (proper mono VIO)

Scale recovered from accelerometer excitation × angular parallax in tracked features. Works in confined spaces (TUM-VI: ATE 5 cm). At 80–130 m altitude over flat ground: feature motion ≈ pure translation → epipolar geometry degenerate → scale blows up. Not a bug, not a tuning knob — a geometric limit.

Path 2: Depth-scale (baro/rangefinder prior)

Use known altitude to set metric scale. Breaks over non-flat terrain: Isaac Sim Cesium terrain depth is ~6× the baro height-above-takeoff (terrain falls away under the drone). Baro constrains z only — once horizontal scale diverges, the chi² gate rejects every baro update.

Key discovery (2026-06-24): Isaac Sim VIO did initialize and lock scale below 20 m after fixing init_max_disparity: 80→1.5 (dynamic init only fires when disparity exceeds the threshold; the smooth climb never produced 80 px disparity). The init fix is correct and kept — the scale divergence above 20 m is a separate observability problem.

Init Bug Fixed

init_max_disparity: 80 → 1.5
Dynamic init fires only when disparity exceeds the threshold. At 80 px, a smooth ascent never triggered it. At 1.5 px, gravity+bias+velocity lock on first camera motion. Keeper fix.

Baro Updater Added

UpdaterBarometer: y = p_z + noise, H=[0,0,1]. Constrains z only. On real flights breaks because chi² gate rejects updates once horizontal scale has diverged. Not a solution for scale — a z-stabilizer only.

Frontend — Feature Tracking Study

Which Tracker Keeps VIO Alive?

4-stage study on MARS-LVIG nadir aerial + TUM-VI handheld. VIO needs ≥ 15 correspondences per frame — min matches and track survival are the critical numbers.

KLT — fastest, best for smooth cruise

~3 ms track-only. 98% survival over 30 frames on nadir. Min=73 at gap 10 — above floor. Breaks on fast motion (min=1 at gap 10 on TUM-VI). Native regime: slow smooth flight.

ALIKED+LightGlue — most robust overall

~17 ms extract+match. 99.8% inlier ratio on nadir. Floor never drops below VIO minimum on either dataset. Tail risk: collapses on featureless frames (0 matches). Best general-purpose VIO front-end.

XFeat+LGdyn — pairwise rescue only

~27 ms. Highest raw count on fast motion. But 65% inlier ratio on nadir and track chain dead by frame 18. Re-detection architecture incompatible with long MSCKF tracks.

Flow-Odom — Working Solution

Altitude-Scaled Optical Flow

Metric-VIO alternative for the nadir cam. Scale comes from height, not IMU. Works where monocular VIO can't.

Nadir Frame N
→

ENU velocity → integrate

How it works

Per frame pair: LK tracks features → normalize with pinhole K → de-rotate by IMU attitude → solve Z·du = -tx + x·tz (per-feature ground depth Z from AGL + attitude ray/ground intersection) → integrate in ENU.

Depth source is the limiter

Baro: scale ~0.28 (4× small) because Cesium terrain is 6× deeper than baro a.g.t. Final error 66 m.
True AGL: triangulate features with GT poses → scale 1.14, final error 22.8 m = 2.2% of 1 km path.

Best result: stride 5 + AGL

Dataset: Isaac Sim 20260624_2337, 1773 m / 450 s.
Stride 5 (every 5th frame at 30 fps → ~5 px flow): 4.6 m = 0.26%, scale 0.98.
No ground truth (AHRS gyro+accel): ~1.5% over 1.8 km.

Next: Rangefinder / True AGL

Replace GT-derived AGL in flow-odom with a real downward rangefinder (lidar/ultrasonic) or terrain DEM. This closes the 0.26% result without any ground truth. Highest leverage hardware add.

Next: FPV Camera (Multi-cam)

Forward FPV camera sees better parallax during cruise. Adding it as a second independent mono stream gives OpenVINS more excitation and breaks the near-pure-translation degeneracy of the nadir cam at altitude.

Next: ALIKED+LG Integration

Integrate ALIKED+LightGlue as OpenVINS front-end (replacing KLT). Benchmark end-to-end ATE on TUM-VI first (known-good baseline), then MARS-LVIG. Expected: survivability improvement on wide-baseline frames.

Bottom line: Flow-odom + AGL delivers 0.26 % accuracy (4.6 m / 1.77 km) — a working GPS-denied solution for steady nadir cruise. Mono VIO (OpenVINS) proves correct on indoor/close-range data but hits a fundamental observability wall above ~20 m. The pipeline and infrastructure are solid; the remaining limits are all hardware/data: a rangefinder for AGL, the FPV camera for parallax, or a low-altitude re-record.
